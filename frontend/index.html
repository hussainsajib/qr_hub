Frequently Asked Questions

Is QR Hub free to use?

Yes, completely free. No account required, no watermarks, no download limits.

What types of QR codes can I create?

QR Hub supports seven types: URL (links to any website), WiFi (lets people join a network by scanning), vCard (saves a contact to your phone), SMS (opens a pre-addressed text message), Email (opens a pre-addressed email), Text (displays plain text), and Phone (initiates a call).

How do I create a WiFi QR code?

Select the WiFi type, enter your network name (SSID) and password, choose the security type (WPA/WPA2 is most common), and generate. Anyone who scans the code will be prompted to join the network automatically — no typing required.

What is a vCard QR code?

A vCard QR code encodes contact information (name, phone, email, organisation, website). When scanned, most phones offer to save the details directly to the address book — ideal for business cards.

Do QR codes expire?

QR codes generated here are static — the data is encoded directly in the image. They never expire and work indefinitely, as long as the destination (URL, phone number, etc.) remains active.

What size should my QR code be for print?

For business cards, a minimum of 2 cm × 2 cm (around 0.8 inches) is recommended. For posters or signage viewed from a distance, scale up proportionally — a code viewed from 3 metres should be at least 9 cm × 9 cm.

What is box size?

Each QR code is made up of small square modules. Box size controls how many pixels wide each module is. A higher value produces a larger, higher-resolution image useful for print. A lower value produces a smaller file suitable for web use.

Can I use these QR codes commercially?

Yes. There are no restrictions on how you use the generated QR codes.
