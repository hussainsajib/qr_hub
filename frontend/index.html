Frequently Asked Questions

Is QR Hub free to use?

Yes, completely free. No account required, no watermarks, no download limits.

What types of QR codes can I create?

QR Hub supports seven types: URL (links to any website), WiFi (lets people join a network by scanning), vCard (saves a contact to your phone), SMS (opens a pre-addressed text message), Email (opens a pre-addressed email), Text (displays plain text), and Phone (initiates a call).

How do I create a WiFi QR code?

Select the WiFi type, enter your network name (SSID) and password, choose the security type (WPA/WPA2 is most common), and generate. Anyone who scans the code will be prompted to join the network — no typing required.

What is a vCard QR code?

A vCard QR code encodes contact information (name, phone, email, organisation, website). When scanned, most phones offer to save the details directly to the address book — ideal for business cards.

Can I add my logo to a QR code?

Yes. Open the Customize panel, upload your logo (PNG, JPG, SVG — up to 512 KB), and it will be centered on the QR code. The error correction level is automatically increased to ensure the code remains scannable.

Do QR codes expire?

QR codes generated here are static — the data is encoded directly in the image. They never expire and work indefinitely, as long as the destination remains active.

What size should my QR code be for print?

For business cards, a minimum of 2 cm × 2 cm (0.8 in) is recommended. For signage viewed from 3 metres, use at least 9 cm × 9 cm. Increase box size in the Customize panel for higher-resolution prints.

Can I use these QR codes commercially?

Yes. There are no restrictions on how you use the generated QR codes.

QR Code Types Explained

URL QR Code

Turn any link into a scannable code — website, landing page, social profile, or app store listing. Ideal for business cards, product packaging, posters, and menus.

WiFi QR Code

Let guests join your network without typing a password. One scan connects them instantly — perfect for cafes, hotels, Airbnbs, and offices running WPA2 or WEP.

vCard QR Code

Encode your contact details — name, phone, email, company, and website — in a single code. When scanned, the phone prompts to save the card to the address book.

SMS QR Code

Open a pre-addressed text message with optional body text. Useful for customer support shortcodes, appointment bookings, keyword campaigns, and feedback forms.

Email QR Code

Launch a compose window with recipient, subject, and body already filled in. Great for contact forms, newsletter opt-ins, and directing feedback to a support address.

Text QR Code

Encode any plain text — coupon codes, serial numbers, instructions, or short messages. The content displays on screen when scanned; no internet connection needed.

Phone QR Code

Encode a phone number so scanning opens the dialler ready to call. One scan, one tap — useful for business signage, print advertisements, and restaurant menus.
